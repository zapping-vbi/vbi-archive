Zapzilla


Description

Zapzilla is the name of the Teletext browser built into Zapping. It is probably the most advanced software Teletext decoder around, at least it's the best one I have seen. ;-) Some of its outstanding features are:
  • Teletext Level 2.5/3.5 ("HiText") support, this includes:
    • Support for a fscking lot of languages, for example Turkish, Russian, Greek, Hebrew and Arabic. Internally we use Unicode.
    • DRCS, i.e. graphics within the Teletext page.
    • 4096 colors, new and enhanced text attributes, a larger character set, more font styles, ...
    • TOP and FLOF navigation.
  • Arbitrary scaling & antialiasing of rendered pages.
  • Regular expression Unicode searching in the page cache.
  • Export filters to HTML, PNG, PPM, ASCII, ANSI, VTX.
  • Wrapping and rectangular text and image selection.
  • Bookmarks.
Those are the pros, there are some cons, though:
  • Due to the scaling, it isn't as responsive as, say, AleVT. It really depends on how big your iron is, and the size of the window. To make a rough estimation, it takes about 0.1s to scale and render a 400x300 page. Not bad for scaling and antialias. But all the credit for this speed/slowdown is due to gdk-pixbuf, I wash my hands. :-)
  • Zapzilla is tied to Zapping. That means tied to all the Gnome libraries. I don't have currently the time/will to untie it myself, but if some brave soul wants to take the challenge, here's my email address. Send petitions to write docs and release libvbi as a separate entity to Michael.

Zapzilla supports searching for regular expressions in the page cache. I gave the "#email#" pattern to search for, the highlighted text is the second match. The window in the back is the help window that appears when you click the "Help" button. And in case you are wondering, "#email#" isn't a regular expression, it's just a convenient alias I added that expands into something as horrific as "([:alnum:]|[-~.]+@([:alnum:]|[-~.])+) ". Basically it matches email adresses. Similarly you can use the "#url#" alias to match WWW adresses, or any regexp you like.

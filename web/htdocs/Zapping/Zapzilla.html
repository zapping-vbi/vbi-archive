Zapzilla

Zapzilla is the nickname of the Teletext browser built into Zapping. Some of its outstanding features are:

  • Teletext Level 2.5/3.5 ("HiText") support, this includes:
    • Support for all Teletext languages, for example Turkish, Russian, Greek, Hebrew and Arabic. Of course this extends to Teletext caption/subtitles.
    • Support for DRCS, permitting graphics in a Teletext page.
    • 4096 colors, new and enhanced text attributes, a larger character set, more font styles, ...
  • TOP and FLOF navigation, all pages stored in memory for quick access.
  • Arbitrary scaling and antialiasing of rendered pages.
  • Regular expression Unicode searching in the page memory.
  • Export to HTML, PNG, PPM, ASCII, ANSI, VTX.
  • Wrapping and rectangular selection with middle mouse button and via clipboard.
  • Bookmarks.

Zapzilla supports searching for regular expressions in the page memory. I gave the #email# pattern to search for, the highlight text is the second match. The window in the back is the help window that appears when you click the "Help" button. And in case you are wondering, "#email#" isn't a regular expression, it's just a convenient alias I added that expands into one horrific expression like "([:alnum:]|[-~.]+@([:alnum:]|[-~.])+)". Basically it matches email adresses. Similarly you can use the "#url#" alias to match WWW addresses.
